The Big Car Count

This app is part of the Connecting Administrative vehicle data for Research on Sustainable Transport (CARS) research project.

https://environment.leeds.ac.uk/dir-record/research-projects/1794/cars-connecting-administrative-vehicle-data-for-research-on-sustainable-transport

The project aims to understand how vehicles are used in the UK and provide better evidence to address a range of issues such as air pollution, congestion, climate change and more.

While we know much about vehicles in the UK a crucial missing piece of information is where the “live”. This app is intended to help build a crowd sourced survey of the home locations of vehicles across the UK. Knowing a vehicles home locations enable a wide range of research for the public good.

How to use this app.

Simply type in the number plate of any parked vehicles you see an click the button that best describes where it is park (e.g. on the road). If you observation has been successful the button will briefly turn into a green tick.

What are we looking for

Vehicles parked in their home locations, the place they go home to a night. So spotting cars on a Sunday afternoon on a residential streets is useful. Spotting cars in a supermarket car park is not useful.

What are we not looking for

Vehicles moving on the roads

Vehicles visiting shops/offices etc.

Delivery vans/taxis/ambulance or any other vehicle that is only temporally in its current location.

Rules and advice for using this app

1) Stay safe. Stay on the pavement away from moving vehicles, don’t go to places that you don’t know.

2) Respect others. If somebody also you not to enter their number plate, respect their wishes. Don’t trespass on private property.

How do you use the data collected.

We will match the number plate to know characteristics of the vehicle (e.g. make/model/engine size/emissions/age). We do NOT match the vehicle to any personal information about the owner of driver.

We use the GPS location of your phone at the time the number plat is observed to identify which neighbourhood the vehicle is parked in. We can then use the data to produce aggregated statistics such as the average vehicle or mileage in different neighbourhoods. The individual observations will not be published and will be deleted at the end of the research project.
